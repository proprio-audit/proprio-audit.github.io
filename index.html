ProprioAudit

A False Sense of State: On the Vulnerability of the Proprioception Channel in Vision-Language-Action Robot Policies

Anonymous Authors
Paper arXiv Code

Abstract

Vision-based attacks on Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models have received increasing attention as these policies are rapidly adopted for robotic control. However, the vulnerability of non-visual inputs, particularly proprioception, remains underexplored. We present ProprioAudit, a framework for studying inference-time adversarial attacks and defenses targeting the proprioception input channel of VLAs. Our attack leverages the velocity field of the VLA action head to optimize small proprioception perturbations that induce significant task failures across representative VLAs in both simulation and real-world settings. We introduce a defense based on stochastic proprioception conditioning, which gives the model an internal mechanism for comparing proprioception-conditioned and proprioception-independent action predictions. We show that this structure enables accurate and rapid detection of adversarial proprioception inputs. Our results show that proprioception conditioning introduces a largely undefended attack surface, and the multimodal structure of VLAs can be leveraged as an effective defense mechanism.

Attacking Proprioception

We adapt the attack optimization formulation from prior work on attacking vision-based diffusion policies, and apply it to the proprioception input channel of VLAs. We implement two variants that differ in how the adversarial objective is constructed.

Single-Step (SS) Attack

Optimizes a perturbation to disrupt the VLA's velocity prediction at a single, randomly-sampled denoising step. Requires no ground-truth demonstrations. The policy's clean prediction serves as the target.

End-to-End (E2E) Attack

Backpropagates through the entire action generation chain and optimizes a perturbation to maximize deviation from the ground-truth demonstration action. E2E directly targets the final output of the policy.

Key Finding

Contrary to prior work on diffusion-based policies (where single-step attacks are more effective at causing task failure), E2E consistently outperforms SS here. Flow matching's Euler sampler is fully deterministic: with no noise re-injected between steps, an early perturbation compounds along one fixed trajectory rather than being reset. This makes whole-trajectory optimization strictly more effective than targeting a single step.

ProprioAudit Defense

We propose a defense that fine-tunes a VLA policy with stochastic proprioception dropout, teaching it a learned null proprioception embedding. The fine-tuned policy supports two action generation paths: one conditioned on the full proprioception state and one conditioned on the null embedding. At inference time, we continuously compare the velocity predictions of these two paths. A sustained divergence in their magnitude signals an adversarial attack on the proprioception input.

Key Finding

Our proposed defense effectively detects all forms of proprioception perturbation (random, SS, and E2E), outperforming existing approaches across both models and tasks. Critically, detection is not only accurate but fast: attacks are flagged with consistently low detection times.
